Land Subdivision.

Land subdivision is basically having one large piece of land usually with a single title deed and dividing it uniformly into smaller parcels which will have their own individual title deeds.

Subdivision is the divisor of land into two or more plots/lots. Its purpose is to split a large tract of land into smaller ones that are easier to develop and can be developed independently of one another to increase growth and maximize the use of space.

02

Beacon Setting.

In land surveying, a beacon setting refers to the process of establishing a reference point or marker that is visible and identifiable from a distance.

These beacons can be physical markers, such as posts, stones, or other objects, which are placed at precise locations to define property boundaries, guide future surveys, or mark significant points in a surveying project. Overall, beacon setting is crucial for effective land surveying, as it provides reliable points of reference that can be used in various applications, including construction, land development, and legal disputes.

Boundary Indentification

A boundary survey is a process carried out to determine property lines and define true property corners of a parcel of land described in a deed.

It also indicates the extent of any easements or encroachments and may show the limitations imposed on the property by state or local regulations. Performing a boundary survey allows us to confirm existing evidence called for at a boundary corner, and reset markers that were missing or incorrectly placed.
